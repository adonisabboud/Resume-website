About Me

Hello! I’m Adonis Abboud, a Computer Science graduate and mathematics student at the Open University of Israel, with a lifelong connection to music. I play the violin and have a deep interest in Arabic maqām music, which naturally led me to explore the space where mathematics, music theory, and software meet.

Lately, I’ve been spending most of my time building interactive audio tools in the browser, experimenting with microtonal tuning systems, and trying to translate musical ideas into working code. I enjoy starting from theory, turning it into something playable or audible, and refining it until it feels right. I like working on problems that don’t fit neatly into one field, and I learn best by building and experimenting.

Arabic Maqām Builder (53-EDO)

Interactive web app for exploring Arabic maqāmāt using a 53-tone equal temperament (Turkish comma system). Implements microtonal intonation (Rāst / Bayātī / Sīkāh half-flats) and renders sound via the Web Audio API with keyboard playback, drones, and transposition.

Tech stack: JavaScript, Web Audio API, HTML/CSS, GitHub Pages.

Automated Music Transcription (AMT) — Arabic Music (WIP)

Research-oriented project focused on automated audio-to-notes transcription for Arabic music. I am experimenting with end-to-end AMT pipelines, including pitch detection, note segmentation, and evaluation workflows. The project currently integrates and evaluates Spotify’s Basic Pitch as a baseline model, with ongoing analysis of its strengths and limitations when applied to Arabic maqām music.

The long-term goal is to improve alignment with Arabic pitch structures and performance practice, including microtonal intervals (e.g. half-flats), non-equal temperaments, and melodic ornamentation. Planned extensions include training and fine-tuning custom models, exploring alternative neural architectures, and designing evaluation metrics better suited to non-Western musical systems.

Tech stack: Python, audio DSP, NumPy, librosa, evaluation scripts, Spotify Basic Pitch (baseline), PyTorch, TensorFlow, model experimentation & training workflows, Git.
